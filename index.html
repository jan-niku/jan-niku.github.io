SIR on a Network

Abstract â€“ A fundamental component of epidemiological modeling, the continuous SIR model, attempts to model the spread of a contagion using systems of differential equations. These base models rely on an assumption of full, constant interaction between individuals. This assumption may poorly reflect more sparse interaction networks. As literature suggests that small-world networks are good approximations of human relations, we expand SIR to these networks. We compare peak infections of continuous SIR models to those predicted by simulations on small world networks with varying degrees of node clustering. We then minimize a cost function comparing infection peaks between these two models by varying the infection rate in the continuous SIR. We show a compensatory factor exists for this infection rate, allowing infections in a network to be approximated using a much simpler continuous model.
